The Association to Benefit Children

A program that helps children ranging from 0-5 learn critical life skills; including patience, respect, and manners. They also help children with disabilities and provides children ranging from 5-21 with summer camp including hands-on activities. Activities happen mostly in Manhattan.

Click here for more information and begin contributing: https://www.a-b-c.org

Big Apple Greeter

You will serve as a greeter, what you mostly do is take a visitor on a tour of a neighborhood and your job is to show them around. They can request any neighborhood in the five boroughs.

Click here for more information and begin contributing: https://bigapplegreeter.org

City Harvest

A program helps by distributing food out. You can work as someone handing out free food to people in need, deliver food to soup kitchens and food pantries, help package food by sorting them into groups, collect excess food from vendors, or work in the office sending mail and other administrative projects. Based in New York City.

Click here for more information and begin contributing: https://www.cityharvest.org

American Red Cross

A program that helps those in need. You can take a leadership role and help with marketing and fundraising, recruit blood donors, help during a natural disaster, educate others on how to prepare for these disasters, help patients in military hospitals, first aid and cpr, etc. You can volunteer for all these types of services and give back to your community.

Click here for more information and begin contributing: https://www.redcross.org/

Want to be rewarded?

Cash in your community service hours to earn rewards ranging from something as a small as a pencil all the way to a gift card!

  • Requirements
  • Rewards

REQUIREMENTS

In order for your community service hours to count, you must present a letter from your supervisor confirming that you have indeed perfomed the correct amount of service hours

Failure to present said letter will result in any potential redeemable hours being voided.

REWARDS

When you redeem your built up service hours, you can expect to earn rewards like:

  1. Pencils
  2. Binders
  3. Notebooks
  4. Bookbags
  5. Gift Cards

Get in Touch

We're always looking for feedback on our website and services, feel free to contact us if you feel we should improve on something.

We also provide free consultation on what types of community service you can expect or should do.

If you feel that you want to perform acts of community service but aren't confident of your skill, feel free to contact us for training. We'll provide the necessary resources to get you up to speed.

Be aware, if you want to schedule a consultation or training session, you must provide a date at least two weeks away from the day of submission.
